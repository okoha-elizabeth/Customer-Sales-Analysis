# Customer-Sales-Analysis
This project analyses customer sales data using SQL to identify key drivers, top-performing products, customer spending patterns, and sales channel performance. It provides insights into business performance and supports data driven decision making through structured SQL queries and business-focused insights.

**Project Overview**
This project analyses customer sales data to identify revenue trends, top-performing products, and customer purchasing behaviour using SQL.

**Tools Used**
•	SQL Server
•	SQL

**Business Questions**
This analysis aims to answer key business questions from the sales dataset:
1. Which products generate the highest revenue for the company?
2. Which product categories contribute the most revenue?
3. Who are the top customers by total spending?
4. How does revenue change across months?
5. Which sales channel is most frequently used by customers?
6. Which sales channel generates the highest revenue?
7. What is the average age of customers purchasing products?
8. Which products generate the highest profit?
9. Which region contributes the highest revenue?
10. What is the average customer rating across product categories?
    
**Key Insights**

1. Laptop Pro emerged as the top revenue-generating product, indicating strong demand for high-value electronics.
2. The Electronics category generated the highest overall revenue, suggesting that technology products are the primary driver of sales.
3. Emma Johnson ranked as the highest spending customer, highlighting the presence of high-value customers that significantly contribute to total revenue.
4. January recorded the highest revenue, suggesting strong sales activity at the beginning of the year.
5. The Store sales channel was both the most frequently used and the most profitable channel, indicating that in-person purchases remain the dominant sales method.
6. The average customer age is 43, suggesting that the business mainly attracts middle-aged consumers with higher purchasing power.
7. The Galaxy A07 generated the highest profit (68,612) within the Electronics category, making it one of the most valuable products for profitability.
8. The average product rating across categories is 4, indicating generally positive customer satisfaction with the products offered.

**Recommendations**

1. Increase inventory and marketing efforts for high-performing electronics products, especially top revenue drivers like the Laptop Pro.
2. Expand promotion of high-profit products such as the Galaxy A07 to maximize profitability.
3. Since store purchases dominate sales, improving in-store customer experience and promotions could further increase revenue.
4. Identify and retain high-value customers like Emma Johnson through loyalty programs or targeted marketing campaigns.
5. Maintain product quality to sustain the high average customer rating (4), which reflects strong customer satisfaction.
